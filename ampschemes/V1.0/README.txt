Tiled primers for RSV were based off previously designed primers (Daniel M Maloney et al., 2023) and PrimalScheme (Quick et al., 2017). The primer sequences were tested against representative RSVA (hRSV/A/USA/WA-UW-76NDB/2022|EPI_ISL_16132384|2022-10) and RSVB (hRSV/B/USA/MA-Broad_MGB-13588/2022|EPI_ISL_16132380|2022-11-08).

Primers were examined to see if they bound to both genomes in a similar position, one genome or both genomes in multiple positions. If any primer bound to both genomes they were considered for the final set if they were binding in similar spots across both genomes. Single binding primers were examined to see if they could be easily modified to work across both genomes. Single primers that bound to one genome with no off-target binding were included in the final primer set. Nine primers were removed from the original scheme and new primers designed using Primer3 in Geneious with the Tm changed to a min 59.7°C, optimum 60°C and max 61°C, size of primer changed to min 20nt, optimum 23nt, max 27nt. All other characteristics remained at default. After generating the new primers, the primer with the closest Tm to 60°C not impacting the current primers whilst maintain an average amplicon length of approximately 400bp was selected as the best and used in the scheme. A total of 178 primers were used; eleven entirely new primers, 19 primers which bound to both genomes and 149 unique single-genome primers
